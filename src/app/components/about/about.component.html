Engineer Baby Steps - 2015/2018

I studied Telecommunications Engineering in the University of Vigo, Spain. It is close to my previous home in Pontevedra and the degree is nationally recognized there, so I thought it would be the right place to study.

Spanish degrees are four years long (240ECTS), being the first one in any engineering just math, physics and some basic programing. The second year has a bit of each of the tracks you can choose for the next two years: telematics, telecommunication systems, image and sound, and electronic systems. I chosed the former which is the area of expertise that deals with the things that are between a software engineer and the transmission of the information through the physical layer. That means, a telematic has knowledge of software engineer system development, informatic transport protocols, data flow and a good background in mathematics, physics and electronics.

Every good story needs a bit of confusion - 2019

I love physics and, when I was close to the end of my studies I started to wonder what would have happened with my life if I had studied physics instead of telematics. I always loved technology but I am quite fascinated about astrology too. Being the inquisitive person that I am I came to the conclusion that I would rather fail miserably than to be all my life wondering what could have been. So, I finished my degree and, because I love travelling, I applied to Groningen University physics program, I studied English hard to get the Cambridge Advanced Certificate and made a jump of faith in August 2019.

And well, I realized I love physics but I don't really need the university anymore. I already have a bachelors degree and the discipline that I didn't have at 18 so, if I want to learn something the best way to go is to learn it myself. I am quite independant in that sense and a little guidance from time to time is all I need. I started to miss technology again and I didn't want to spend 3-5 more years studying. I wanted to start making a difference, so, in January 2020, I decided that even though I love physics, I want to continue being an engineer.
